## Who Needs a Commercial License?

If you wish to use Uni-CLI in a proprietary product or service **without** complying with the AGPL-3.0 obligations (i.e., without open-sourcing your modifications), you need a commercial license.

### You DO NOT need a commercial license if:

- You use Uni-CLI as a CLI tool (running commands, using in scripts)
- You build open-source projects that use or extend Uni-CLI
- You write YAML adapters and contribute them back
- You use Uni-CLI internally within your organization without distributing modifications

### You DO need a commercial license if:

- You embed Uni-CLI's code into a closed-source commercial product
- You offer Uni-CLI (or a modified version) as a hosted service without open-sourcing your changes
- You redistribute modified versions of Uni-CLI without making source available
